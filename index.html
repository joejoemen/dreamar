Chicago
Beautiful Chicago was rescued by DREAM Animal Rescue from the East Valley Shelter in Los Angeles. She had been dumped there with an enlarged abdomen. It was determined she had a large mass on her spleen and her body was filling with fluid. She would die without treatment. After consulting with our emergency veterinarian, it was determined that the only way to save her life was to remove the spleen. there were concerns that the mass could be cancer and her life expectancy would be cut short. During the surgery it was discovered that some of the lymph nodes were also not looking normal so they were removed as well. Her surgery and recovery all went as expected and she is doing great. About a week later we received the results on the biopsies, the mas on the spleen was cancer. However the lymph nodes were clear. It is determined that she has mast cell lymphoma. This is a slower growing cancer and there’s not much treatment that can be done. We don’t know what her future hods or how long she will live. Looking at the cases similar to hers, life expectancy has been anywhere from 1 to 5 years. We are hoping its the latter. We would love to find her a forever home where she can live out her years in happiness and comfort. She has made a full recovery from surgery and is playing and acting like a kitten so we are hoping this is a good sign. Please help us in covering the costs for here surgery as they exceeded $4,600.
Kleo
Female Meet Kleo. A very well-behaved lap kitty that loves being petted, being held and lovvvvvvvves her bed (foster mom always covers her with soft blankets). She is very humble and not picky at all. The most easy-going girl with the best personality! Gentle, sweet and such a loveable girl! Kleo just melts in your arms. She gets along with other cats after a slow introduction but can be the only kitty in the house as well. Some people say that Kleo has very sad eyes and they make her look grumpy, but that is not the case at all! She is a very happy kitty and loves to play and loves everything about life! *She has had a faint positive test for FIV and we are waiting to re-test her, after a few months. She can live with other cats, she can live with dogs, there are no restrictions.
